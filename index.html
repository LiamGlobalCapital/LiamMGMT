FAQ

Common questions.

What does a mother agency do?

A mother agency is the primary agency that discovers, develops, and represents a model or creator. We handle career strategy, professional development, and place talent with larger destination agencies in major markets worldwide. Think of us as the home base. While our talent may be signed to additional agencies in cities like Paris, Milan, London, or Tokyo, we remain the central management throughout their career.

Who does Liam Mgmt represent?

We represent male models, digital creators, and Muay Thai athletes who cross over into modeling and content. Our roster is intentionally small. We focus on individuals with a distinct point of view, strong work ethic, and long-term potential rather than quick placements.

How do I apply to be represented?

Submit your application through our Get Scouted form. We review every application personally. If we see a fit, someone from our team will reach out within two weeks. We do not require professional photos, formal training, or prior experience to apply.

What is the difference between a mother agency and a destination agency?

A destination agency is one that books work in a specific market, like New York or Paris. They handle bookings within that city. A mother agency, like Liam Mgmt, manages the whole career. We coordinate with destination agencies on behalf of our talent, set strategy, negotiate contracts, and protect our talent's long-term interests across every market they work in.

Do you charge upfront fees?

No. Liam Mgmt never charges fees to apply, audition, or be represented. We earn through commission on bookings we secure for our talent. Anyone claiming to represent us and asking for upfront payment is not affiliated with our agency. Verify by emailing desk@liammgmt.com directly.

Where are you located?

Our headquarters is in New York at 304 Park Avenue South. We have additional offices in Los Angeles, Miami, and Dallas. We work with talent based anywhere and place them with agencies worldwide.

Do I need professional photos to apply?

No. Natural digital snapshots taken on a phone are exactly what we want. Clean face, no makeup, hair pulled back, plain background, natural light. We are looking at you, not at production value.
